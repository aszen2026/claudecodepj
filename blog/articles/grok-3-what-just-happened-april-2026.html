Grok 3: What Just Happened (April 2026)

We tested the leading grok 3 tools and broke down exactly what works, what doesn't, and which option fits your workflow. No sponsored opinions — just honest analysis.

What Happened

Almost a year after Elon Musk pledged that xAI would open-source Grok 3 "within a few months" of Grok 4's release, the weights are still nowhere to be found. Grok 4 shipped in July 2025. Grok 4.1 followed in November 2025. Grok 4 Fast arrived in September 2025. And as of today, April 28, 2026, the Grok 3 repository on Hugging Face remains conspicuously empty — no checkpoints, no tokenizer, no inference code.

Musk's original commitment, made in March 2025 right after Grok 3 launched, mirrored the playbook xAI used with Grok 1 in March 2024 — a 314-billion-parameter Mixture-of-Experts model released under Apache 2.0 once a successor existed. Grok 2's weights followed in August 2025, roughly a year after that model's launch and one month after Grok 4 shipped, under a more restrictive xAI community license that bars training competing models. The pattern suggested Grok 3 would land sometime in late 2025 or early 2026.

Instead, the silence has stretched past nine months. xAI has not issued a revised timeline, a license preview, or any technical paper covering Grok 3's architecture. Internal priorities appear to have shifted toward Grok 4.1, the new Grokipedia project, and the long-rumored Grok 5 training run reportedly underway on the Colossus 2 cluster in Memphis.

💡
The pattern check: Grok 1 → Grok 2 was 17 months from launch to weight release. Grok 2 → Grok 3 was 12 months. If Grok 3 (Feb 2025) follows the curve, weights should already be out. We are past the precedent.

Why It Matters

Grok 3 isn't just any model. At launch it was xAI's frontier system, trained on roughly 200,000 H100s, and benchmarked competitively against GPT-4o and Claude 3.7 Sonnet. Open weights of a system that strong would have been the largest fully-released frontier model in the public domain — bigger than DeepSeek-V3's 671B MoE, bigger than Llama 3.1 405B in effective compute spent.

The delay matters for three groups specifically:

  • Sovereign-AI buyers — governments and regulated enterprises that committed to xAI partly on the open-weights promise now have a procurement gap. France's Mistral and China's DeepSeek are the obvious fallbacks, and both have been aggressive in 2026.
  • Researchers — academic groups had built planned distillation, interpretability, and red-teaming work around Grok 3 weights. That pipeline is stalled.
  • Musk's credibility on AI commitments — this is the second high-profile AI timeline he has missed in a row, after the Optimus Gen 3 demo slip in February.

The winners are quieter but real: OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google DeepMind all benefit from a frontier-tier open release not landing. Every quarter Grok 3 stays closed is a quarter the closed-API economics on Tier-1 models hold up.

Context

The broader open-weights landscape has shifted under xAI's feet. When Musk made the original promise in March 2025, releasing a 2024-era frontier model in 2025 looked generous. Today, with DeepSeek-R2 (released January 2026) and Qwen 3.5-Max already matching or beating Grok 3 on most public benchmarks at a fraction of the inference cost, the strategic value of releasing Grok 3 has decayed sharply.

That creates an awkward incentive. The longer xAI waits, the less the release matters competitively — but also the less anyone will care, which removes pressure to ship at all. It's the same dynamic that kept Meta's original LLaMA gated and eventually pushed Stability AI's promised models into limbo.

There is also a licensing question. Grok 2's weights came with non-compete restrictions that the open-source community pushed back on hard. If xAI is debating whether to use the Apache 2.0 path (Grok 1) or the community-license path (Grok 2) for Grok 3, that internal disagreement alone could explain months of delay.

💡
Read the silence: xAI hasn't said "delayed" or "canceled" — it has said nothing. In open-source release timelines, silence past a stated window almost always means an internal reversal is being debated, not that the release is imminent.

What To Do Next

  • If you were planning to fine-tune Grok 3: stop waiting. Build on Qwen 3.5, DeepSeek-V3, or Llama 4 405B. The capability gap to a hypothetical Grok 3 release is now small enough that switching costs dominate.
  • If you're a procurement lead at a regulated buyer: get the open-weights commitment in writing in your next xAI contract, with a specific date and a remedy clause. The verbal promise pattern is no longer reliable.
  • If you're tracking Musk's AI roadmap for investment or competitive reasons: watch Grok 5's launch. xAI's historical pattern is to release the n−2 model when n+1 ships. If Grok 5 lands without Grok 3 weights, the open-source promise is effectively dead.
  • If you're a researcher who needs frontier open weights now: DeepSeek-R2 and Qwen 3.5-Max are your near-term ceiling. Plan accordingly — don't burn another quarter waiting on xAI.
